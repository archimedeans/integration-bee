Welcome to the official contest website for Round Two of the 4th ICMC!

If you have not yet done so, please log in and change your password.

Round Two will run during 14:00–17:00, 28 February 2021, GMT, with submission closing at 17:30 GMT. There is an embargo until 18:00, 28 February 2021, GMT. Please review the "Contest details" section below for more information on the format, timing and rules of the contest. Technical information such as how to use the website is available in the "Technical information" section.

Past papers are available on our static-content website, as always.

Contact information

If you encounter technical difficulties on the website at any point or wish to ask a question about the problems during the contest, please don't hesitate to contact us using our web form (log-in required) or by email at contest@icmathscomp.org.

Contest details

This is a closed-book competition. You will have 3 hours to solve 4 problems, arranged roughly in order of difficulty. Each problem carries 10 marks.

The competition will run during 14:00–17:00, 28 February 2021, GMT. There is an embargo until 18:00, 28 February 2021, GMT.

You may choose to type up your solutions in a word processor or TeX editor, but there is no requirement to do so.

At 17:00 GMT, you must stop writing and/or typing and begin preparing your scripts for online submission. You will have 30 minutes after the competition to upload your solutions. Please upload a separate PDF file (up to 5 MB) for each problem you have attempted. Any uploads made after 30 minutes are not guaranteed to be accepted, and we cannot accept any submissions made after the embargo is over.

  • As far as possible, the exam should be taken under test conditions, in a quiet room, with only the permitted materials and a printout of the contest paper if desired.
  • Participants are permitted to use a pen, pencil, highlighter and/or other writing implements, as well as a ruler and compass.
  • Participants are allowed to use an electronic device only for the purposes of accessing the contest website, writing an email to contest@icmathscomp.org for the purpose of clarification, or typing their solutions in a word processor or TeX editor.
  • Please make sure you write your Contestant ID on every page. Do not include other personally identifiable information such as your name.
  • The following are prohibited: calculators of any kind; protractors, set squares, or other geometric tools; books, scripts, documents, websites or other resources; communication with others, particularly online.

Technical information

Contestants can log in with their user credentials by following the "Log in" link in the menu bar (on devices with a smaller screen, you may need to toggle a button in the top-right corner to see the link).

Contestants who have logged in will see links reading "Problems" and "Submission" in the menu bar, which will take you to the corresponding pages.

The "Problems" page is where the problems will be displayed once the contest starts.

The "Submission" link will take you to the "Submission summary" page, where you can see the submission status for each problem. After the contest has started and before submission closes, links for uploading and re-uploading your solutions will be available on the page. There will also be links to the PDFs you have uploaded.

This website has been tested in the latest versions of Chrome, Edge and Firefox. It may also work as intended in other modern browsers. It is unlikely to work in Internet Explorer.

If you are a contestant and you believe you have encountered any technical issues without immediate solutions, please get in touch with us using the contact details above as soon as possible.

This website uses session cookies for user authentication and security purposes. They are stored on your device so that this website can identify you (or more precisely, your browser instance) as a previous visitor to the website and keep track of the website user you have logged in as.
