About

The UK University Integration Bee is an inter-university team-based integration competitions, run by The Archimedeans alongside mathematics societies at Oxford, Warwick, Durham and Imperial.

The competition is in two parts: an online round and an in-person final.

In Round 1 (the online round), you will receive a collection of integrals which you need to solve as a team. Answers will then be inputted into our online autograder. While we can't say for sure what solving the integrals will involve, you will find some helpful information below to help prepare!

In Round 2 (the in-person final!) teams who did sufficiently well in Round 1 will be invited to St John's College Cambridge to compete in a three part competition, featuring group, crossintegral and shuttle rounds. More information will follow after Round 1!

Syllabus & Contest Information

Round 1 will take place on Sunday, 17 October 2021 between 2pm and 4pm.

There will be 40 problems, covering a wide range of difficulty and integration techniques. There will be both definite integrals (where only the numerical answer matters) and indefinite integrals.

To get a feel for the type of techniques that may be involved in the problems, we have written a syllabus which you can get here. The best way to practice is looking at problems from previous competitions, which you can find below.
